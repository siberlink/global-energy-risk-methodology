# What Is Energy Risk? A Complete Guide to Geopolitical and Market Risk in Energy

Energy risk refers to the **probability that geopolitical events, supply disruptions, or systemic stress factors will impact energy markets, prices, and supply stability**.

It is a critical concept for:

- Energy traders  
- Macro investors  
- Policy makers  
- Analysts and researchers  

---

## Why Energy Risk Matters

Energy markets are highly sensitive to external shocks.

These include:

- War and geopolitical conflict  
- Sanctions and trade restrictions  
- Infrastructure disruptions (pipelines, LNG terminals)  
- [LNG Supply-demand imbalances in Europe](https://energyriskiq.com/data/europe-lng-supply-demand) 
- Market sentiment and volatility
- [Energy Risk Forecaster](https://energyriskiq.com/data/global-energy-risk-forecast) 

Even small disruptions can lead to **large price movements and systemic instability**.

---

## Types of Energy Risk

### 1. Geopolitical Risk
Events such as war, sanctions, or political instability that impact energy flows.

### 2. Supply Risk
Physical disruptions in oil, gas, or electricity systems.

### 3. Market Risk
Volatility driven by sentiment, speculation, or macroeconomic shifts.

### 4. Structural Risk
Long-term vulnerabilities such as low storage levels or supply dependency.

---

## The Problem with Traditional Analysis

Most energy analysis focuses on:

- Prices  
- Supply-demand balance  

However, these are **lagging indicators**.

Markets often move **before fundamentals change**, driven by perceived risk.

---

## The Need for Risk Indices

To better understand energy markets, risk must be **quantified independently**.

This is where structured indices come in:

- Global Energy Risk Index (GERI)  
- European Energy Risk Index (EERI)  
- Europe Gas Stress Index (EGSI)  

These indices track risk in real-time, providing **leading indicators** for market behavior.

## Key Insight

Energy markets are not just driven by supply and demand.

They are driven by **expectations of disruption**.

# Geopolitical Risk Framework for Energy Markets

Geopolitical risk is one of the most powerful drivers of energy markets.

This framework explains how geopolitical events translate into **measurable energy risk**.

---

## What Is Geopolitical Risk?

Geopolitical risk refers to:

- War and armed conflict  
- Sanctions and trade restrictions  
- Political instability  
- Strategic competition over energy resources  

These events directly impact:

- Oil supply
- [European LNG Supply & Demand](https://energyriskiq.com/data/europe-lng-supply-demand)
- [Gas storage levels in Europe](https://energyriskiq.com/gas-storage-levels-in-europe)  
- Energy infrastructure  
- Market confidence  

---

## The Energy Transmission Mechanism

Geopolitical events affect energy markets through several channels:

### 1. Supply Disruption
Direct loss or restriction of energy supply.

### 2. Infrastructure Risk
Damage or threat to pipelines, terminals, or transport routes.

### 3. Market Sentiment
Increased volatility and speculative behavior.

### 4. Policy Response
Sanctions, export bans, or emergency interventions.

---

## From Event to Market Impact

The process typically follows:

Event → Risk Signal → Market Expectation → Price Movement

Understanding this chain is critical.

---

## Quantifying Geopolitical Risk

Traditional analysis is qualitative.

Modern approaches require **quantification**.

This is achieved through structured indices:

- Global Energy Risk Index (GERI)  
- European Energy Risk Index (EERI)  
- Europe Gas Stress Index (EGSI)  

---

## Key Insight

Geopolitical risk does not impact markets instantly.

It accumulates, builds pressure, and then triggers reactions.

---

# Why Energy Prices Fail to Capture Real Risk

Energy prices are widely used as indicators of market conditions.

However, they often **fail to reflect underlying risk**.

This creates blind spots for traders, analysts, and decision-makers.

---

## The Core Problem

Prices reflect:

- Current market balance  
- Short-term sentiment  
- Immediate supply-demand conditions  

They do NOT fully capture:

- Future disruptions  
- Escalating geopolitical risk  
- Structural vulnerabilities  

---

## Key Limitations of Price-Based Analysis

### 1. Lagging Response
Prices often react **after events escalate**, not before.

### 2. Sentiment Noise
Markets overreact or underreact based on short-term sentiment.

### 3. Hidden Risk
Systemic stress builds beneath the surface without immediate price impact.

### 4. Policy Distortion
Government interventions can temporarily suppress price signals.

---

## Real-World Example

Markets can remain stable while:

- Conflict intensity increases  
- Supply routes become vulnerable  
- Storage levels decline  

This creates a **false sense of stability**.

---

## Risk vs Price

| Factor | Price | Risk |
|---|---|---|
| Timing | Reactive | Forward-looking |
| Signal | Noisy | Structured |
| Sensitivity | Short-term | Systemic |

---

## The Solution: Risk-Based Frameworks

To understand markets properly, risk must be measured directly.

This is the role of:

- Global Energy Risk Index (GERI)  
- European Energy Risk Index (EERI)  
- Europe Gas Stress Index (EGSI)  

These provide a **structured view of underlying conditions**.

---

## Key Insight

Markets move not just on reality, but on **perceived future risk**.

# Global Energy Risk Index Methodology (GERI, EERI, EGSI)

A transparent, research-driven framework for quantifying **global energy risk, geopolitical escalation, and system stress** across energy markets.

This repository provides detailed methodology, models, and interpretation frameworks behind the EnergyRiskIQ indices:

- Global Energy Risk Index (GERI)
- European Energy Risk Index (EERI)
- Europe Gas Stress Index (EGSI)

---

## Why This Exists

Energy markets are increasingly driven by **risk before fundamentals**.

Traditional models rely on:
- Price movements
- Supply-demand balance

But markets often move **before those signals appear**.

This framework is designed to:

→ Quantify geopolitical escalation  
→ Measure systemic energy stress  
→ Translate complex signals into actionable risk levels  

---

## Core Indices

### GERI — Global Energy Risk Index
Measures global systemic energy risk using:

- Conflict intensity
- Supply disruption signals
- Cross-market contagion
- Global escalation patterns

---

### EERI — European Energy Risk Index
Focuses on risk transmission into European energy markets:

- Regional geopolitical pressure
- Energy system exposure
- Market-sensitive escalation dynamics

---

### EGSI — Europe Gas Stress Index
Tracks structural stress in the European gas system:

- Storage deficits
- LNG flow dynamics
- Pipeline risk and chokepoints
- Market volatility

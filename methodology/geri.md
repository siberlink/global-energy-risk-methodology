# Global Energy Risk Index (GERI) — Methodology

The **Global Energy Risk Index (GERI)** measures systemic energy risk at a global level, capturing how geopolitical events, disruptions, and market signals translate into potential stress across energy systems.

---

## What GERI Measures

GERI is designed to quantify:

- Global geopolitical escalation
- Energy supply disruption risk
- Cross-market contagion (oil, gas, freight, FX)
- System-wide stress signals

Unlike price-based indicators, GERI focuses on **risk conditions before markets fully react**.

---

## Core Components

GERI is constructed using a weighted aggregation of:

- **Event Severity**  
  War, sanctions, infrastructure attacks, major disruptions

- **Geographic Impact**  
  Regional vs global significance

- **Energy Exposure**  
  Degree of impact on critical energy systems

- **Market Transmission**  
  Likelihood of impact on oil, gas, and macro markets

- **Persistence**  
  Duration and compounding nature of risk signals

---

## Scoring

All inputs are normalized into a **0–100 scale**:

- 0–20 → Low  
- 21–40 → Elevated  
- 41–60 → High  
- 61–80 → Severe  
- 81–100 → Critical  

---

## Interpretation

- Rising GERI → Increasing global systemic risk  
- Falling GERI → Risk normalization  
- High GERI + stable prices → Market underpricing risk  

---

## Use Cases

- Energy market risk monitoring

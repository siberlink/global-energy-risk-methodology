# European Energy Risk Index (EERI) — Methodology

The **European Energy Risk Index (EERI)** measures escalation risk specific to European energy markets, capturing how global and regional developments impact Europe’s energy system.

---

## What EERI Measures

EERI focuses on:

- Regional geopolitical pressure on Europe
- Energy supply vulnerability (gas, LNG, pipelines)
- Transmission of global risk into EU markets
- Market-sensitive escalation signals

---

## Conceptual Framework

EERI is derived from three primary layers:

- **Regional Escalation Pressure**  
  Conflict and geopolitical developments affecting Europe

- **Theme Pressure**  
  Energy-related narratives (supply cuts, sanctions, disruptions)

- **Asset Transmission**  
  Impact on key assets such as TTF gas, LNG flows, and infrastructure

---

## Key Insight

EERI is not just a subset of global risk.

It measures **how risk is transmitted into Europe**, which can diverge significantly from global conditions.

---

## Scoring

Normalized on a **0–100 scale**:

- 0–20 → Normal  
- 21–40 → Elevated  
- 41–60 → High  
- 61–80 → Severe  
- 81–100 → Critical  

---

## Interpretation

- EERI rising faster than GERI → Europe-specific stress  
- High EERI + low storage → structural vulnerability  
- Divergence vs prices → sentiment mismatch  

---

## Use Cases

- European gas and power analysis  
- TTF market context  
- Policy and risk monitoring

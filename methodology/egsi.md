# Europe Gas Stress Index (EGSI) — Methodology

The **Europe Gas Stress Index (EGSI)** measures structural stress within the European gas system, combining physical constraints and market signals.

---

## What EGSI Measures

EGSI captures:

- Gas storage levels and refill dynamics  
- LNG import dependency and flow variability  
- Pipeline risks and geopolitical chokepoints  
- Market volatility (TTF and related indicators)  

---

## Core Components

EGSI is built from multiple stress pillars:

- **Supply Stress**  
  Availability of gas supply sources  

- **Transit Stress**  
  Pipeline and transport risks  

- **Storage Stress**  
  Storage levels vs seasonal norms  

- **Market Stress**  
  Volatility and pricing signals  

- **Policy Stress**  
  Regulatory and geopolitical interventions  

---

## Scoring

Each component is normalized and combined into a **0–100 index**:

- 0–20 → Low stress  
- 21–40 → Moderate stress  
- 41–60 → High stress  
- 61–80 → Severe stress  
- 81–100 → Critical stress  

---

## Interpretation

- High EGSI → Fragile gas system  
- Rising EGSI → Increasing vulnerability  
- High EGSI + low storage → elevated winter risk  

---

## Key Insight

Gas markets often price stress late.

EGSI is designed to act as a **leading indicator of structural vulnerability**.

---

## Use Cases

- European gas market analysis  
- Storage and refill monitoring  
- LNG and supply chain tracking  
- Risk-aware trading strategies
